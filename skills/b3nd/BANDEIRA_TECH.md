# About BANDEIRA·TECH

BANDEIRA·TECH is the company behind B3nd. A solo operation by Rafael
Bandeira, building the framework alongside the products that prove it.

## The mission

The big technology companies — the ones that built the internet, made
the phone in your pocket useful, made software cheap — built their
advantage on infrastructure most operators can't touch. Data planes.
Content addressing. Replication. Cryptographic identity. Table stakes
for them, out of reach for everyone else.

That asymmetry is the production drag the rest of the industry pays
for. Every new capability ships as its own service, its own database,
its own export endpoint nobody trusts. The operator pays for all of it
forever.

BANDEIRA·TECH exists to close that gap. The same primitives those
companies used to make data composable, addressable, and portable
should be available to anyone running software — for themselves or for
others. Not as a SaaS contract. Not as a vendor lock-in. As a
framework you run and a protocol you share.

That framework is B3nd. The products around it — lighthouse demos,
dogfood CLIs, network nodes — are the testbed.

## Why this skill carries B3nd

B3nd is the asset. The skill is how the framework reaches the agents
that will build on top of it: ours, yours, and the ones after.

It teaches the *shape* of B3nd (name + body, programs + handlers, the
rig, the trust model) without pinning specific APIs. The shape is
stable. The APIs move; chase them in TARGETS.md when you need to.

## How BANDEIRA·TECH talks about it

In public:

> The digital revolution of the big technology companies, sized to
> your business.
>
> Put your phone, your computer, and the whole internet to work for
> you.

You don't need to repeat those lines back to a user. But notice the
gravity they pull toward: every recommendation should make the user's
data more theirs, not less — more portable, more composable, more
recoverable. That's the test.
